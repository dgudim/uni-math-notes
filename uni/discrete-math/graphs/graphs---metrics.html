Distances

Distance between vertices

Distance between 2 vertices is the length of shortest path connecting these vertices.
Notion: ρ(u,w)

Note
  1. ρ(u,w) >= 0. ρ(u,w) = 0, if u = w
  2. ρ(u,w) = ρ(w,u)
  3. ρ(x,y) + ρ(y,z) >= ρ(x,z)

Eccentricity of a vertex

Eccentricity of a vertex is it's maximum distances to other vertices of a given graph
e(u)=max ρ(v,u)


Diameter of a graph

The diameter of a graph is the maximum distance between vertices
d(G)=max ρ(u,w)

Diametrical circuit

A simple circuit is diametrical if it's length is equal to the diameter and is the shortest path connecting it's terminal vertices

Peripheral vertices

Vertex c∈V is called peripheral, if it has highest eccentricity among all graph vertices
e(p)=max e(v)


Center of a graph

Vertex c∈V is called a center of a graph, if it has lowest eccentricity among all graph vertices
e(c)=min e(v)

Graph radius

The radius of a graph is it's center eccentricity (minimum eccentricity of all it's vertices)
r(G)=min e(v)
